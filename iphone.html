Pool Fence Barrier

Is the barrier a minimum of
1200 mm high measured around
the outside of the pool fence/ barrier?



As per AS1926.1-2012 (Section 2.2.1) the height
and radius arc of operation of the Non Climbable
Zones(NCZ) 1,2,3,4 & 5 around the pool fence barrier
shall be not less than 900 mm
except NCZ4 in their relative quadrants. Within the NCZ
there shall be no handholds or footholds, objects or
plants greater than 10mm in size that will facilitate climbing.

Are all the non-climbable zones free of objects?



As per AS1926.1-2012 (Section 2.3.1) there are to be no steps,
retaining walls or level changes within 500 mm of the barrier.

Does the barrier maintain a 500 mm exclusion zone?



Are all the gaps around the pool fence or barrier less than 100 mm?



Is there a child resistant barrier in good repair
between any residential building structures,
which also include fixed BBQ's, clotheslines, sheds, garages,
cabanas, games rooms, lounge areas and
the like, but not shade structures, and the swimming pool?



Do all structural components show evidence of passing
requirements for the strength and rigidity of openings?



As per AS1926.1-2012 (Section 2.2.2) the pool fence/ barrier
with openings greater than 10 mm must not
have any objects in the 300 mm x 900 mm non-climbable
zone on the inside of the pool fence.

Does the pool fence have a 300 mm clear zone on the inside?



-------------------------------

Gate/s

As per AS1926.1-2012 (Section 2.4.3) hinges within NCZ
must not protrude out from the barrier with
a horizontal top surface depth greater than 10 mm and
have an opening between the gate post and
the gate stile of more than 10 mm.

Are the hinges at least 900mm apart if they have
a 10 mm or more surface depth within NCZ?



Does the pool gate swing outwards, away from the pool
area uninterruptedly to a stop position
whereby it can close by itself?



Is the gate self-closing and self-latching from
any position in the full arc of operation?



Is the pool latch 1500 mm from the finished ground
level or is the latch at least 150 mm from
the top of the gate located on the inside of the barrier?



-------------------------------

Boundary Barrier

Is the boundary fence 1800 mm minimum
in height measured from the top rail to
ground level on the inside of the barrier?



Is there an adequate 900mm NCZ located
on the inside of the boundary barriers/fences,
with maximum 10mm projections (footholds/handholds)
within the relevant NCZ?



Is the width of an internal barrier that intersects
a boundary barrier that is within the 900mm NCZ
not more than 50mm wide or is
the internal barrier 1800mm high?



-------------------------------

Mesh, Windows and Balustrades

As per AS1926.1-2012 (Section 2.3.2) Low barriers (not boundary) using perforated
mesh not less than 1200 mm high, the openings
in the material must be no larger than 13 mm and can
the barrier sustain a lift of 100 N without leaving
a gap of more than 100 mm at the bottom and
can it support a weight of 25 kg without
reducing the height to below 1200 mm?




As per AS1926.1-2012 (Section 2.3.2) High barriers (not boundary) using
perforated mesh are all the openings no
larger than 100 mm if not less than 1800 mm high
and can the barrier sustain a lift of 100 N without
leaving a gap of more than 100 mm at the
bottom and can it support a weight of 25 kg
without reducing the height to below 1800 mm?




Is the window opening either restricted to a 100 mm opening,
have a metal screen or bars fixed to the
window or is the window ledge at least 1800 mm high
from the finished ground level?




Does any balconies located within 1800mm of the finished ground level
of a pool barrier, comply with pool fencing requirements?




Does the balustrading of any balconies located within the 900mm NCZ
of a pool barrier, comply with pool fencing requirements?




-------------------------------

Safety Sign

As per section 17 of the Swimming Pools Act 1992,
a current resuscitation (CPR) sign must be
installed which includes updated details (Sept 2019)
of DRSABCD which can be easily read
from a distance of 3 metres.

Act 1992 Section 22E a notice of noncompliance stating the certifier
is of the opinion that the pool does pose a significant risk to public safety.

Is the pool located on the property deemed a significant risk to public safety?
